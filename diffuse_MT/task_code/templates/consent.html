University of Texas at Austin, Department of Psychology

You have been invited to take part in a research study about decision-making. We need your consent to proceed.

The purpose of this study is to examine individual differences in decision making and learning. The study is being conducted by Nathaniel Blanco under the supervision of W. Todd Maddox, PhD, of the Department of Psychology at The University of Texas at Austin. You can contact Nate at nathanblanco@utexas.edu to talk about the study or ask questions.

What will be done if you take part in this study?

• View a series of images on a computer screen and make choices between different options

• Demographic information will also be asked.

Compensation: You will be compensated $1 for your participation in this study. After completion of the experiment, your data will be analyzed. If accepted, payment will go through.

Risks: There are no known risks to participating in this experiment.

Confidentiality of Data: Any work performed on MTurk can be linked to the user’s Amazon.com public profile page. Thus, workers may wish to restrict what information they choose to share in their public profile. MTurk worker IDs (i.e., the 14 character sequence of letters and numbers used to identify workers) will be collected for the purpose of providing compensation, but MTurk worker IDs will NOT be shared with anyone. MTurk worker IDs will be removed from the data set. More information regarding Amazon’s Mechanical Turk privacy policies can be found here: https://www.mturk.com/mturk/privacynotice. If it becomes necessary for the Institutional Review Board to review the study records, information that can be linked to you will be protected to the extent permitted by law. Your research records will not be released without your consent unless required by law or a court order. The data resulting from your participation may be made available to other researchers in the future for research purposes not detailed within this consent form. In these cases, the data will contain no identifying information that could associate it with you, or with your participation in any study.

Participation or Withdrawal: Your participation is voluntary. You can decide not to be in the study at all. If you start the study, you can stop at any time by just closing your browser window. Stopping or not being in the study won’t affect your relationship with The University of Texas at Austin in any way. However, you will not receive payment unless you complete the study in its entirety.

Contacts: Please contact Nathaniel Blanco at nathanblanco@utexas.edu regarding any questions, concerns or comments regarding the online experiment. For more general inquiries, Todd Maddox, PhD or a member of his research staff is available at maddoxlab@psy.utexas.edu to answer any other questions. This study has been reviewed and approved by The University Institutional Review Board and the study number is 2009-04-0090.

Questions About Your Rights As A Research Participant: If you have questions about your rights or are dissatisfied at any time with any part of this study, you can contact, anonymously if you wish, the Institutional Review Board by phone at (512) 471-8871 or email at orsc@uts.cc.utexas.edu.
